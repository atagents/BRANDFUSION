## 📐 PART 1: MASTER PROMPT ENGINEERING FRAMEWORK

### The 8-Element Cinematic Formula
Every high-performance prompt must include ALL 8 elements:

| Element | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject** | Who/what is on screen | A bearded mechanic welder |
| **Action** | Active movement (video NEEDS motion) | arc welding overhead, sparks cascading |
| **Emotion/Mood** | The feeling to evoke | intense focus, raw industrial power |
| **Environment** | Where the scene takes place | dark ship hull interior, ocean dock |
| **Camera Movement** | How the camera moves | slow dolly-in from wide to medium close-up |
| **Lighting** | Time of day + light sources | harsh sodium floods, sparks as natural light |
| **Style Keyword** | Cinematic tier + format | 4K, shallow DOF, cinematic, IMAX-grade |
| **Negative Elements** | What to avoid | static, blurry, overexposed, subtitles |

### Platform-Specific Prompt Modifiers

**For TikTok (9:16 Vertical — 15-30s)**
Append: `vertical 9:16 format, fast cuts, high contrast, vibrant saturation, scroll-stopping hook in first frame, strong motion in opening seconds`

**For YouTube Shorts (9:16 — up to 60s)**
Append: `vertical 9:16 format, educational aesthetic, clear subject, informative overlays possible, strong CTA moment at end`

**For Instagram Reels (9:16 — 30-90s)**  
Append: `polished premium aesthetic, aspirational tone, luxury color grading, smooth slow motion moments, emotionally resonant`

**For YouTube Long-Form (16:9)**
Append: `cinematic widescreen 16:9, epic establishing shots, deep narrative mood, broadcast quality aesthetic, 4K minimum`

---

### Negative Prompt (Universal — Use for ALL Models)
```
static frame, blurry details, overexposed, oversaturated, subtitles, text overlays, watermark, painting, illustration, worst quality, low quality, JPEG artifacts, deformed face, extra fingers, fused limbs, motionless background, gray tone, washed out, cluttered frame, duplicate subjects, morphing faces, flickering, temporal inconsistency
```

## 🧠 PART 2: VIRAL PSYCHOLOGY FRAMEWORK

The 6 Psychological Triggers That Cause Shares (2026 Research):

### Trigger 1: The 3-Second Shock Hook
The opening frame must trigger one of these:
- **Visual Impossibility** — Something that physically cannot exist
- **Emotional Sucker Punch** — Immediately evokes a strong feeling
- **Curiosity Gap** — Creates a question only the video can answer

### Trigger 2: Beautiful Absurdity
In 2026, audiences have developed immunity to "hyper-realistic" AI. What works instead:
- Take a **normal concept** and make it **visually impossible**  
- Example: A cat giving a TED talk. An octopus doing sushi prep. A medieval knight using an iPhone.
- Rule: The MORE absurd the better, as long as one element feels grounded and real.

### Trigger 3: The Human Sandwich (Anti-AI-Slop Rule)
**Human insight → AI production → Human edit**
- YOUR unique angle/idea is the foundation
- AI does the visual rendering and music
- You make the final emotional resonance call
- Generic "AI Slop" now gets actively suppressed by algorithms

### Trigger 4: FOMO Framing  
Every caption and hook must signal "this is happening right now and you're missing it." 
- "Nobody is talking about this yet..."
- "The most insane [X] I've ever seen..."
- "This just changed everything for [NICHE]..."

### Trigger 5: Platform-Native Patterns
- **TikTok:** Deliberately imperfect looking, trending audio, "one more take" energy
- **YouTube Shorts:** Curiosity-based titles, educational value implied even in entertainment
- **Instagram Reels:** Aspiration, beauty, luxury aesthetic — make them feel like they're missing out

### Trigger 6: The First 3 Frames Rule
Design the first 3 frames of your video specifically to stop a scrolling thumb:
- **Frame 1:** Visual shock or strong emotion
- **Frame 2:** Confirms the hook — makes them lean in
- **Frame 3:** Preview of the payoff — makes them stay
